Dear Periannan Chandrasekaran,

the word gu?i is normally used for 'temple' in the early Telugu
inscriptions.

K. M. Sastri (Historical Grammar of Telugu, p. 68) refers to SII X, 709
(k?yilala) but the text has kayilala (~ kavilala 'cows'), so it is probably
a misprint.

The inscription that you refer to (e. g. IA 13, pp. 50-55) has
peddak?yilamu 'old temple' but it occurs in the delineation of a grant and
may hence rather be an individual name.

Apart from that I am not aware of another early inscription that has the
words k?yila or k?vela. If I happen to come accross another instance I will
let you know.

Best wishes,
Jens
