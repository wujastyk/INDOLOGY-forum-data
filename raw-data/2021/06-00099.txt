Dear Paolo,

These look to me like images of ritualized instruction of a sacred text, probably a tantra/?gama; the object on the ground between the ascetic on the left (presumably the teacher/?c?rya) and figures on the right appears to a bookstand (called a yogap??ha in chapter 1 of the Pi?gal?mata). One chapter of Jinah Kim?s new book Garland of Visions: Color, Tantra and a Material History of Indian Painting (UC Press) illustrates several examples of these (I don?t have the book at hand, but the relevant pages should be easy to locate).

Best wishes,
