Times of India, 21 Sept 2012:

NAGPUR: After a successful pilot run, the Central Board of Secondary
> Education (CBSE) has decided to introduce a new elective course for Std XI.
> The course known as Knowledge Traditions and Practices of India<http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/topic/Knowledge-Traditions-and-Practices-of-India>will be available to students from this academic session under the elective
> course code no. 073.
>
> CBSE's circular says that the newly introduced course has "received an
> overwhelming response from educationists and the teaching community all
> over India". It aims to engage students with a sense of exploration and
> discovery while highlighting the concepts and major achievements in various
> disciplines of knowledge.
>
> This elective is keeping in line with the board's endeavour to develop the
> curriculum in various subjects with a deep focus on values inherent in
> Indian education system. This focus further takes shape in facilitating
> knowledge of traditional practices through the course material developed
> for students.
>
> The various modules of this course have already been put online and a
> printed book version is currently under process. The board is also
> preparing a Hindi version of the course. The board has written to
> principals requesting them to include this elective in their scheme of
> studies. There are a total of 10 modules being offered by CBSE<http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/topic/CBSE>in this course.
