*PhD Position in Buddhist Studies*

*Vacancy number: 12-213*

The Leiden University Institute for Area Studies (LIAS) invites
applications for two fulltime PhD positions in the field of Buddhist
Studies, specialization open, to begin 1 January 2013, or thereafter. **

Since its foundation in 1575, Leiden University has built an
internationally recognised record of excellence in teaching and
research. Currently, the University has around 17,000 students and
4,000 staff from around the world. The Faculty of Humanities consists
of the Institutes for Area Studies, Creative & Performing Arts,
Cultural Disciplines, History, Linguistics, Philosophy, and Religious
Studies. The Graduate School has an annual output of about 50 PhDs.

The Leiden University Institute for Area Studies
(http://www.hum.leiden.edu/lias/), which comprises the Schools of
Asian Studies and Middle Eastern Studies, is committed to the
integration of disciplinary and regional-historical perspectives, and
has as its aim the advancement of teaching and research of Area
Studies at Leiden University and in the wider academic community. Area
specializations in Asian Studies include Chinese, Japanese, Korean,
South- & Southeast Asian, and Tibetan Studies.
